In the 60s, I met Louis Dixon in a bar where he saw I was doodling a figure on a napkin. He offered me a drink and made small talk, but I was too engrossed in my work. Frustrated, he handed me a five dollar bill, took the napkin, and shook my hand. I never saw him or my drawing again. The image of it slowly faded from my mind, until one day, the visage haunted me among the aisles in ShopRite, Paramus. There I saw it again, on the shelf, but tenfold. Some five bucks that was.
